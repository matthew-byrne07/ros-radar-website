02 Description of Service

ROS Radar is a mobile application designed to assist self-employed individuals, sole traders, and businesses in the Republic of Ireland with the organisation and classification of financial receipts and expenses for the purposes of Irish Revenue Self-Assessment (Form 11).

The App provides:

  • Receipt image capture and AI-powered data extraction
  • Expense categorisation aligned with Irish Revenue Allowable Trading Expenses
  • VAT3 Reclaim tracking
  • Capital Allowances record keeping
  • Form 11 Self-Assessment expense ledger management

ROS Radar is a productivity and record-keeping tool only. It does not constitute the provision of professional tax, legal, or financial advice.

04 Data Retention — Section 887 TCA 1997

Under Section 887 of the Taxes Consolidation Act 1997, taxpayers are legally required to retain all business records, receipts, invoices, and supporting documentation for a minimum of six (6) years from the end of the tax year to which they relate.

In accordance with this obligation:

  • All receipt images, extracted data, and transaction records stored in ROS Radar are retained for a minimum of six (6) years from the date of upload
  • Hard deletion of financial records within the statutory retention period is blocked at the database level
  • Users are advised not to rely solely on ROS Radar as their only method of record retention. We recommend retaining original paper receipts or maintaining duplicate digital copies in accordance with Revenue guidance

Failure to maintain adequate records may result in Revenue imposing estimates of tax liability under Section 889 of the Taxes Consolidation Act 1997.

05 Allowable Trading Expenses & Audit Guard

The App classifies expenses into the following Irish Revenue Form 11 trading expense categories:

  • Office Costs & Administration
  • Rent & Utilities
  • Travel & Subsistence
  • Advertising & Marketing
  • Professional Fees

Certain expense categories are automatically flagged as "Disallowed" in accordance with Irish Revenue rules, including but not limited to:

  • Client entertainment
  • Fine dining expenses
  • Personal gym memberships or non-business personal expenses

This Audit Guard feature is provided as a protective tool only. The final determination of whether any expense is an Allowable Trading Expense rests with the Revenue Commissioners and, where disputed, the Tax Appeals Commission. The App's classifications do not constitute a formal Revenue ruling.

06 VAT and VAT3 Reclaims

ROS Radar tracks VAT amounts extracted from receipts to assist with VAT3 Return preparation. This functionality is provided for organisational purposes only.

VAT reclaim eligibility is governed by the Value-Added Tax Consolidation Act 2010 and related Revenue Commissioners guidance. Not all VAT amounts identified by the App will be reclaimable. You are responsible for:

  • Verifying your VAT registration status with Revenue
  • Confirming that individual expenses are eligible for input VAT recovery
  • Filing accurate VAT3 Returns with the Revenue Commissioners
  • Maintaining VAT records for the statutory six-year period

Irish VAT rates applied by the App (Standard 23%, Reduced 13.5%, Second Reduced 9%, Zero-Rated 0%) are correct as of the date of this agreement. VAT rates are subject to change by the Minister for Finance in the annual Budget. Users should verify current applicable rates.

07 AI-Powered Data Extraction

ROS Radar uses a third-party AI vision model (OpenAI GPT-4o Vision) to extract financial data from receipt images. The following conditions apply:

  • AI extraction is not infallible. Extracted values including amounts, dates, merchant names, and tax figures must be verified by you before reliance for tax purposes
  • Receipt images are transmitted to OpenAI via a secure server-side function with data retention logging disabled. No personally identifiable information (PII) is included in API payloads
  • OpenAI processes data under its own terms of service and applicable data protection law. We have configured our integration to opt out of training data usage
  • You must not upload receipts containing sensitive information beyond standard business expense records. Do not upload documents containing PPS numbers, bank account details, or medical information
